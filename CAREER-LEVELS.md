# Career Levels — How to Read This Lab

Both **de-lab** and **devops-lab** tag every challenge with a **target career level**. This is not gatekeeping — it's so you know what interviews and job titles each exercise maps to.

| Badge | Level | Typical experience | What it means here |
|-------|--------|-------------------|-------------------|
| 🟦 | **Intern / Trainee** | 0–6 months | Follow steps, learn vocabulary, complete with hints |
| 🟩 | **Junior** | ~6–18 months | Do it hands-on, explain *what* you did |
| 🟨 | **Middle** | ~2–4 years | Design trade-offs, debug without hints |
| 🟥 | **Senior** | 4+ years | Own architecture, cost, reliability, stakeholders |

## Two axes (don't confuse them)

| Axis | Example | In this repo |
|------|---------|--------------|
| **Quest level** | 🟢 L1 SQL, 🟡 L2 Python | Order of the story — do in sequence |
| **Career level** | 🟩 Junior, 🟨 Middle | Who this challenge prepares you for |

You can be **Junior in career** but on **Level 3 of the quest** — that's normal when switching from analytics to DE.

## de-lab focus (important)

This lab is **Data Engineering with product analytics DNA**:

- You should know **why** a metric exists, not only how to `SELECT` it
- Pipelines serve **product, finance, and growth** — not "tables for tables"
- **Analytics Engineer** skills (dbt, semantic layer) are included — but the **north star** is **DE** (reliability, scale, ownership)

See [`interview-sprint/AE-vs-DE.md`](../interview-sprint/AE-vs-DE.md) for role differences and salary reality.

## devops-lab focus

Infrastructure ownership: ship, automate, observe, recover. Interview sprint covers Linux, Docker/K8s live scenarios, and system design.
